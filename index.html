APOL1 and Its Origin

Everyone has a gene called APOL1 or apolipoprotein L1. We each have two copies of the APOL1 gene, one inherited from each of our parents.

Some people may have changes in the APOL1 gene called mutations or variants that increases their risk of developing chronic kidney disease.

The APOL1 gene variants originally evolved in people who lived in Western and Central Africa, to protect them from a deadly disease known as African sleeping sickness. Over time, these same gene variants are now known to increase a person's risk or likelihood of developing kidney disease.

APOL1 Kidney Disease

APOL1 kidney disease, also known as APOL1-mediated kidney disease or AMKD, refers to chronic kidney disease in patients with the APOL1 gene variants and is associated with various types of chronic kidney diseases.

APOL1 kidney disease is a genetic condition that people can inherit from their family because it is passed down through genes. APOL1 kidney disease impacts people globally.

Changes (mutations or variants) in the APOL1 gene can lead to kidney damage. Having the APOL1 gene variants can increase the chance of an individual losing kidney function faster.

Recognizing Symptoms

Kidney diseases can show up in different ways. Symptoms for APOL1 Kidney Disease are similar to other kidney diseases. Some people may have one or more signs or symptoms that something is wrong, but others may not have any until their kidney disease is very severe. That is why it is important to see a doctor and check their kidney function with simple urine and blood tests.
